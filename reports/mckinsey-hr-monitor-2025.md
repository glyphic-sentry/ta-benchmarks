## Retention Drivers — Employee View vs. HR View

Critical gap: HR and employees rank the same factors very differently.

| Retention Factor | Employee Rank | HR Rank | Gap |
|-----------------|--------------|---------|-----|
| Job security | #1 — 39% | #3 — 32% | HR undervalues by 7 pts |
| Work–life balance | #2 — 34% | #2 — 38% | Roughly aligned |
| Relationships with colleagues | #3 — 33% | #6 — 18% | HR undervalues by 15 pts |
| Flexibility | #4 — 31% | #4 — 30% | Aligned |
| Compensation and benefits | #5 — 28% | #1 — 41% | HR overvalues by 13 pts |
| Training and development | #9 — 17% | #5 — 25% | HR overvalues |

**Key insight:** HR is over-investing in compensation and training as retention tools while dramatically underestimating the importance of colleague relationships and job security to employees.

## Hybrid Work

- Employees work **2.3 days remotely per week** on average (EU + US)
- **38% of employees want to increase** their remote days
- Top reasons for wanting to work from home: better work-life balance (61%), reduced commuting (61%), flexibility in hours (49%), productivity (33%)
- Top reasons for wanting to work on-site: social connection/collaboration (52%), better equipment (40%), clearer work-life separation (38%)

## Key Findings

McKinsey's HR Monitor 2025 reveals five systemic gaps between what HR delivers and what employees need. The most consequential: **HR and employees rank retention drivers dramatically differently**. HR puts compensation at #1; employees put it at #5. Employees put colleague relationships at #3; HR ranks it #6. This misalignment means retention budgets are going to the wrong interventions. Offer acceptance rates across Europe average just 56% — far below US benchmarks (~77–81%) — and the combined effect of low acceptance and 18% probationary attrition leaves only 46% of candidates still employed six months after offer. Quiet quitting risk is high in France, Italy, and Spain, where dissatisfaction rates are more than twice attrition rates. The feedback gap is striking: 26% of European employees received no feedback in the past year, but HR estimates only 6% — a 20-point perception gap that directly undermines development and retention. Gen AI adoption in HR is two times higher in the US than Europe (35% vs. 19% operational deployment), and US employees receive formal gen AI training at twice the rate of European counterparts.
