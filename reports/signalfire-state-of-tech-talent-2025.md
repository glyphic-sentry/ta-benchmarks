## Entry-Level & New Grad Hiring Collapse

| Segment | New Grad % of Hires | YoY Change | vs. Pre-Pandemic (2019) |
|---------|-------------------|-----------|------------------------|
| Big Tech (top 15 by market cap) | 7% | ↓25% from 2023 | ↓50%+ |
| Startups (Top 100 VC, Seed–Series C) | <6% | ↓11% from 2023 | ↓30%+ |
| Magnificent 7 specifically | — | — | New grad share dropped by >50% since 2022 |

**The experience paradox:** Companies post junior roles but fill them with senior ICs. Root causes: end of "free money" low-rate era (2020–2022), tighter budgets, shorter runways, AI automating routine entry-level tasks, and the lean team philosophy.

## SignalFire's Predictions for 2025

1. **Rise of the generalist engineer** — Copilot, Cursor, Replit maturing means generalists who move fast beat deep specialists. Companies will prioritize collaborative flexibility over narrow expertise.
2. **Year of the equity advisor** — Lean startups hire fractional CMOs, CFOs, CTOs on equity. Carta data shows these roles are more affordable than before.
3. **New job categories emerge** — AI governance lead, AI ethics/privacy specialists, agentic AI engineers, non-human security ops. Early-career talent should watch these.

## Key Findings

The tech talent market in 2024–2025 is undergoing a structural reset, not just a cyclical downturn. The most consequential trend: **new grad hiring has collapsed** — down 50%+ at Big Tech and 30%+ at startups from pre-pandemic levels, with no sign of recovery. This is driven by the end of the low-rate era, AI automating routine tasks, and leaner team philosophies (Series A startups are 20% smaller than 2020). The **AI lab retention race** is the clearest talent story: Anthropic's 80% 2-year retention and ~95% offer acceptance rate — while poaching at 8:1 and 11:1 ratios from OpenAI and DeepMind — shows mission and culture beating comp as the primary retention lever. **Geographic reconsolidation** is underway: SF and NYC are recovering their dominance (65%+ of AI engineers), while Austin and Houston cool sharply. Miami and San Diego are the fastest-rising challengers. Toronto is also named as an emerging hub.
