## The 4 Key Shifts

### 1. Operations Hiring in Decline
- Operations hiring rate fell 20% to 27%
- Lowest salary increase eligibility (14% vs. 23% overall)
- Lowest promotion rates (3.4%)
- Bifurcation happening: routine ops tasks being automated; strategic ops work being elevated or absorbed into other functions
- Despite decline, Operations still has a 27% hiring rate — replacement demand remains high

### 2. Entry-Level Hiring Has Collapsed
- P1/P2 hiring rates down 73% year-over-year
- Junior roles in People, Marketing, and Engineering hardest hit
- Drivers: AI automating routine/repetitive tasks; economic caution; lean team philosophy
- Long-term risk: mid-level and leadership bench strength gaps in 3–5 years

### 3. AI Hiring Gold Rush
- AI/ML new hires grew 88% in 2025 vs. 2024
- Distinct AI/ML job titles grew 50% in one year
- AI/ML Engineer = 45% of all AI/ML titles; Senior AI/ML Engineer = 15%
- AI pay premium: 12% for Professional track, 3% for Management track
- Premium is moderate at median; extreme packages concentrated at frontier labs

### 4. Early-Stage Startups Hiring More Selectively
- All funding stages converged to a narrow 27–30% band (vs. 28–49% spread in 2022)
- Early-stage down 35% — founders opting for leaner, more intentional teams
- Focus on AI-native talent and customer-facing roles (e.g., Forward Deployed Engineers)
- Goal: maximize impact per headcount, not grow headcount for its own sake

## Key Findings

Tech hiring has stabilized at 29% — the same rate as 2024 — but beneath the surface, the structure of hiring has shifted dramatically. AI/ML roles grew 88%, while entry-level hiring collapsed 73%. Operations is undergoing structural transformation: high replacement demand but declining new role creation as automation reshapes what the function looks like. All funding stages have converged on similar cautious hiring rates, with early-stage companies pivoting from rapid headcount growth to lean, high-impact team building. Commercial is the anomaly — the only function where both hiring increased and attrition decreased — reinforcing the strategic priority of revenue-generating roles.
