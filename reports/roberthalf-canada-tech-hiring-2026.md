## Key Legislative Note: Ontario Pay Transparency Act

Effective **January 1, 2026**, Ontario's Pay Transparency Act requires all employers to disclose salary ranges in job postings. This applies to all Ontario-based job advertisements and materially changes how compensation is communicated in the hiring process. TA teams hiring for Toronto roles must comply.

## Key Findings

Nearly half (48%) of Canadian tech hiring managers plan to increase headcount in 2026, but only 5% feel they already have sufficient skills on their teams — signaling a wide talent gap. The most acute gap is in AI and machine learning (cited by 42% of leaders), followed by governance/compliance and infrastructure. Despite strong intent to hire, 50% of leaders say hiring skilled talent is harder today than a year ago. The dominant response is upskilling: 69% say reskilling current employees is necessary to meet 2026 targets. Soft skills are increasingly weighted in hiring decisions — critical thinking (67%) and adaptability (65%) top the list of competencies sought to complement AI tooling. Canada's tech hiring market remains competitive but more measured than the US, with generally lower comp benchmarks offset by quality-of-life advantages and strong AI research infrastructure (particularly in Toronto and Montreal).
