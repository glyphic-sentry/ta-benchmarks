## Key Findings

Benefits strategies in 2025 reflect an industry at an inflection point. Health, leave, and retirement remain the top-three benefits by importance (88%, 81%, 81% respectively) and have been stable for years. The story is in what's emerging and what's fading. **GLP-1 drug coverage** is the breakout benefit of 2025 — 23% of employers now offer it, with significant cost management challenges ahead. **AI chatbot subscriptions** (ChatGPT, Copilot, Claude.ai) are offered by 16% of employers, reflecting a new category of productivity-enablement benefit. **Standalone wellness benefits** (flu vaccinations, health assessments, smoking cessation) have declined sharply since 2021 — employers are consolidating or cutting these. **Flexible work** continues to pull back slightly: hybrid work fell 3 points to 60%, open PTO is down to just 6%. **Family care benefits** are retreating across the board — dependent care FSAs, bereavement leave, and paid family leave all declined. Elder care remains dramatically under-provided (7–13%) despite an aging workforce. The retirement story is positive: 401(k) match averages 6.3%, and Roth 401(k) adoption has surged 12 points since 2021.
