## Key Findings

Open and reply rates are declining industry-wide — sequences sent in 2025 saw open rates drop 1.3% and reply rates drop nearly 6% year-over-year, reflecting increasing candidate fatigue and inbox saturation. Personalization is the highest-leverage lever: subject lines including company, first name, and title outperform generic subject lines by over 10 percentage points. Shorter is almost always better — 3-word subject lines and 51–100 word email bodies dramatically outperform longer alternatives, with one notable exception: SMB candidates respond better to longer, more detailed messages. Send-on-behalf-of (SOBO) is underutilized (only 19.2% adoption) but delivers outsized lift, especially after Stage 2 silence (+61.3% reply rate). Timing shifted in 2025: Wednesday at 3pm replaced Tuesday at 8am as the optimal send window, and follow-up spacing has compressed — candidates now respond faster when contacted 4 days after the first email rather than 6.
