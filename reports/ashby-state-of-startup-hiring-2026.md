## Source of Hire by Startup Size

| Source | Smallest (<25) | Larger (100–300) |
|--------|---------------|-----------------|
| Inbound | Largest source | Largest source |
| Sourced | 30% of hires | Lower share |
| Referrals | Lower than avg | ~15% (vs. 18% all companies) |

Startups rely more heavily on sourcing than larger companies, especially at the earliest stages. Referrals are slightly less common at startups (15%) than across all company sizes (18%) — counter to the common assumption that early-stage networks drive most hires.

## Recruiter Impact on Time-to-Hire

| Stage | Effect of Recruiter Involvement |
|-------|-------------------------------|
| <25 employees | ~30% reduction in time-to-hire |
| 25–300 employees | Consistently faster, but gap narrows |

At the smallest startups, adding a recruiter to a job cuts time-to-hire by nearly a third. As startups scale, the hiring architecture built by TA teams benefits all jobs — even those without a recruiter explicitly assigned.

## AI Adoption in Startup Talent Teams

- 60% of startup Ashby customers using at least one AI feature (Q3 2025)
- Most use 1–2 features; 10% use 5+ features (fully AI-integrated recruiting)
- Larger startups adopt more AI features — likely driven by higher volume and coordination complexity
- Most common AI use cases: sourcing personalization, feedback summaries, application review, AI note-taking
- AI usage has increased every quarter since Q1 2023

## Key Findings

Startup hiring in 2025 was defined by scale, speed, and AI adoption. Over half (60%) of startup talent teams now use AI in their recruiting workflows — no longer experimental, but embedded across sourcing, screening, and coordination. The biggest structural insight: **remote roles dramatically outperform in-office roles at every funnel stage** — 42% more inbound applications and 9% higher offer acceptance rates — yet the share of remote-friendly startup jobs has fallen from 80% to 60% since 2023. This creates a significant competitive sourcing advantage for startups that maintain remote flexibility. Recruiter ROI is most dramatic at the earliest stage: for startups under 25 employees, involving a recruiter cuts time-to-hire by ~30%. Referrals are less common at startups (15%) than the industry average (18%), with sourcing playing a disproportionately large role — especially for the smallest teams where 30% of hires come from sourcing. Offer acceptance rates are stable at ~80%, in line with the all-company benchmark.
