## Manager Impact on Engagement

- Managers account for **70% of the variance in team engagement**
- Only **44% of managers** have received any formal training
- Trained managers improved their own engagement by **22%** and their teams by up to **18%**
- Global manager engagement fell from 30% → 27% in 2025

The manager gap is the single largest structural problem in the engagement data: managers drive the majority of engagement outcomes, yet the majority have had no formal training to do so.

## Recognition & Belonging — ROI Data

Employees who feel recognized and included are:
- **3× more likely** to describe their workplace as fun

Companies with strong recognition practices see up to a **21% uplift in profitability** (Gallup).

## Key Findings

Global employee engagement hit 21% in 2025 — the sharpest drop since the COVID-19 lockdowns and well below the 2023 peak of 23%. Disengagement now costs the global economy an estimated $8.9 trillion annually (~9% of GDP). The root cause Gallup identifies is not pay or benefits, but **management quality**: managers account for 70% of engagement variance, yet fewer than half have received formal training. Manager engagement itself is falling fastest — especially among younger managers (under 35) and female managers, who saw a 7-point drop. Wellbeing indicators are also deteriorating: 41% of employees reported significant daily stress, only 34% describe themselves as thriving, and 1 in 5 felt lonely on a given day (rising to 1 in 4 for remote workers). Europe is the most disengaged region globally at 13%, with the UK at a stark 10%. The implication for TA and retention: engagement is a stronger predictor of employee departure than compensation, and the lever is management investment.
