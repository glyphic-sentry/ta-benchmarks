## Key Findings

Toronto is Canada's dominant tech hub with ~400,000 tech professionals and #1 North America ranking for tech job growth among mid-sized markets. The market is in a "precision hiring" phase — hiring volumes are strong (+12% YoY job postings) but employers have shifted philosophy from "hire fast" to "hire right, even if slow." The AI talent gap is the defining pressure: job postings for AI-adjacent roles grew 62% while qualified applicants grew only 18%, pushing ML engineer time-to-hire to 41 days. A notable reversal from 2022–23: remote roles now pay 3–5% less than hybrid positions in the GTA, as employers increasingly require 2–3 days onsite — particularly in FinTech and HealthTech where compliance drives workplace policy. Ontario's Pay Transparency Act takes effect January 1, 2026, requiring salary ranges in all job postings — this will materially change how compensation is communicated in hiring processes.
