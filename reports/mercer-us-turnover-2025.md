## Key Findings

US voluntary turnover has continued its post-Great Resignation decline, reaching 13.0% in 2025 — down sharply from 17.3% in 2023 and a further improvement from 13.5% in 2024. This marks a significant normalization following the hyper-attrition period of 2021–2023. The pattern of higher positions having lower turnover (executives at 5.2% vs. para-professionals at 12.5%) suggests that investment in leadership retention pays disproportionate dividends. Industries with the highest turnover (Retail & Wholesale at 26.7%) are primarily driven by lower pay, fewer benefits, and hourly workers with more job mobility. Roles that are hardest to fill — healthcare, logistics, specialized services — require proactive retention management rather than reactive backfill.
