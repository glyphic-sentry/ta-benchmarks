## US Labor Market Context (Feb 2026)

- 6.9M job openings vs. 4.8M hires — a persistent conversion gap
- Unemployment: 4.3% (Jan 2026), with 130K nonfarm payroll additions
- SHRM forecasts "low-hire, low-fire" environment; monthly payroll growth projected at 55,200 jobs (vs. 125,100 in 2025)
- 2025 job creation revised down 69% by BLS (181K actual vs. 584K initially estimated)

## AI in Recruiting

- 43% of organizations used AI for HR/recruiting in 2025, up from 26% in 2024 (SHRM)
- Among publicly traded companies: 58% AI adoption
- Top use cases: job descriptions (66%), resume screening (44%)
- Only 37% of TA professionals actively integrate GenAI into workflows (LinkedIn) — adoption ≠ integration
- AI-assisted recruiters save ~20% workload (≈1 day/week) — LinkedIn 2025
- Josh Bersin Company: AI-powered TA delivers 2–3x faster hiring cycles, up to 75% efficiency gains
- 60% of candidates abandon overly long applications — AI-streamlined apply flows reduce drop-off

## Skills-Based Hiring

- 70% of employers use skills-based practices (NACE, 2026 Job Outlook), up from 65%
- GPA screening dropped from 73% (2019) to 42% (2025)
- Primary evaluation venues: interviews (87%), resume screening (65%)
- Companies conducting the most skills-based searches are 12% more likely to make quality hires (LinkedIn)

## Remote Work & Recruiting

- Only 11% of Q4 2025 postings were fully remote; 55% of candidates prefer hybrid or remote
- Tech roles: 58% on-site, 29% hybrid, 13% remote — slightly more flexible than overall market
- Seniority gap: senior workers get 30% hybrid + 13% remote; entry-level gets 18% hybrid + 9% remote
- Entry-level candidates getting least flexibility despite being most likely to expect it — creates pipeline friction

## TA Budget & Headcount

- Only 30% of companies expect TA budget growth in 2026
- Only 24% plan to add recruiter headcount
- 59% plan to increase TA technology investment
- Paradox: tech spend growing while headcount stays flat — automation replacing headcount, not supplementing it

## Key Findings

2026 TA operates under a "do more with less" mandate. AI adoption in recruiting nearly doubled year-over-year (26% → 43%), but the meaningful gap is between ownership and integration — only 37% of TA professionals have actually changed how they work. The labor market is in a "low-hire, low-fire" state: 6.9M openings but only 4.8M hires, with payroll growth forecast to drop by more than half. TA budgets are flat, recruiter headcount is flat, but tech investment is surging — companies are explicitly replacing headcount with automation. Skills-based hiring hit 70% adoption, driven by the collapse of GPA screening (73% → 42% since 2019). The remote work gap creates a structural sourcing problem: only 11% of postings offer fully remote while 55% of candidates prefer it, and entry-level workers get the least flexibility despite expecting it most.
