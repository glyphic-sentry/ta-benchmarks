## Funnel Stage Passthrough Rates

- **Recruiter screen**: ~35% passthrough (higher for referred: 52%)
- **Post-onsite**: ~95% passthrough
- **Offer stage**: ~81% acceptance

## Company Size Context (~500 employees)

- First RecOps hire typically made between 200–500 employees
- At ~100 employees, Time to First Fill begins to stabilize — inflection point for structured planning
- 500+ employees: scorecard completion ~72% (vs. 49% for <25 employees)
- Smaller orgs have wide TTF distribution; larger orgs show narrower, more predictable ranges
- As orgs scale, variability in hiring timelines narrows — consistency precedes speed improvements

## Role Complexity Impact

- Senior roles take 37% longer to fill than junior positions
- Technical roles take ~15 days longer to fill than business roles
- Technical roles: ~17.6 interviews per hire (up 52% since 2021)
- Business roles: ~11.7 interviews per hire (up 36% since 2021)

## Referral Source Performance

- 52% of referred candidates pass initial screens (vs. 35% overall)
- Referrals have higher passthrough at nearly every stage
- Referrals from same job function outperform cross-function referrals significantly
- Referred candidates have higher offer acceptance than both inbound and sourced

## Time Delays in Hiring (Top Bottlenecks)

1. Application review (compound delay for high-volume tech roles)
2. Scheduling — lead time is bigger bottleneck than scheduling speed itself
3. Feedback / decision-making — gap between final interview and decision
4. Offer approvals — multi-stakeholder approval chains

## Key Findings

Hiring performance is a system problem, not a single-bottleneck problem. Companies with faster overall time-to-hire tend to be faster at *every* stage by a small margin, not dramatically faster at one stage. The 26% scheduling speed advantage from automated scheduling compounds across multi-stage loops. Scorecard alignment matters: 38% of scorecard pairs include at least a 1-point difference; nearly half of those cross the yes/no threshold (2 vs. 3 on Ashby's 1–4 scale), which increases decision time.
